Research

My research primarily focuses in robot perception (using computer vision and wearable sensors) and cognitive science for applications within Human-Robot Interaction (HRI). Currently I am working on a perception project for Honda Research Institute's robot prototype Haru in which we are creating a computer vision algorithm to pair with non-invasive physiological sensors (such as a thermal camera) to detect engagement in children interacting with the robot. Another project I am involved in is alongside IU's Computer Vision lab and Toyota Research Institute. We are using LuxAI's QT robot to create interactions with elderly adults to help them find their "ikigai", a Japanese concept that roughly translates to "purpose for being." The final project I am involved in is in collaboration with Mississippi State University on their Therabot robot for Major Depressive Disorder patients.
