AR Graffiti

"For this project, you will augment a public space or object/thing to make it greater. This should be a space or object/thing close enough for our class to walk to. To make this space/object/thing greater you will research its history/origin to understand its intricacies and relationship to our shared space and culture. Your augmentation could take the form of a digital object, sound, video, game etc. that has some kind of contextual relationship to the thing being augmented."

For this project I created an AR Makerspace. Using lasercut pieces of birch wood and the Vuforia engine I created image targets based on the laser cut pieces and created Blender models of common Makerspace technologies to generate based on the image targets. In Unity I combined the Blender models and the image targets to create an Android app that will use a mobile camera to generate the AR Makerspace.

Hypermedia Mirrors

"I would like you to explore the aesthetic and conceptual possibilities of procedural and/or interactive media. Using the internet as your medium (specifically the p5.js programming environment) I would like you to create a work that explores an idea through interactivity (mouse, keyboard, sound input for example) or through automated procedural steps (e.g. for loops, if/else statements, functions). You can use images, videos and/or sound in your P5 sketches and/or you can create audio/visual media from scratch in P5 as generative art. The topic or theme for this assignment is the nature of the internet. How do we turn it into something unexpected."

For this project I used the ML5.js library to create a style transfer application based on Fast Style Transfer by Reiichiro Nakano. The application only contains two styles from models pretrained by other creative coders working on the fast style transfer algorithm. The two styles available are The Great Wave off Kanagawa by Hokusai and Udnie by Francis Picabia. In the application you can switch between styles, upload the image you want stylized, and then hit the style button. The application will then display your image restyled with the style of the artwork you chose.

Arduino

"For this project, we will be manipulating inputs and outputs. You will learn how to use sensors to gather data from the real world (Input) and use Arduino to react to that data using some kind of media (output). You will choose from a variety of sensors on the circuit playground to gather data to inform how your audience will interact with your artwork. Lastly, you will create a compelling physical object for your circuit to be housed in. You will also have the option of utilizing processing to create a reactive media experience that interprets the data and turns it into further output. How is it used as a symbol and sign? What kinds of meanings does it carry? Think about this work as an installation. How would you design the user's experience through your work?"

For this project I used the Circuit Playground Express's (CPE) buttons and light sensor to allow the user to choose an LED on the CPE and then change its color. The colors of all the LEDs would then be sent to a P5 site where it would be displayed on the screen in a position relative to the rotation of the CPE as given by it's accelerometer. This allows the user to draw pixel art on the screen of the CPE by changing the CPE's colors and rotating it in their hand.
